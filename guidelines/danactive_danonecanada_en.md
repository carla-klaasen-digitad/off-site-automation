Language: Canadian English

---

## Brand Voice

- DanActive is a probiotic drinkable yogurt — position it as a simple, everyday habit, not a medical intervention
- Do not dramatize the digestive health benefit; it should read as a gentle, practical aside

## Product Naming

- Brand name: DanActive (capital D, capital A — written as one word)
- "drinkable yogurt" or "probiotic drink" — acceptable product descriptors
- "fermented dairy drink" — acceptable
- Do not say "yogourt drink" (yogourt is the French spelling; use "yogurt" in English)
- Do not say "probiotic supplement" — DanActive is a food, not a supplement

## Claims

- Probiotic claim rule: always hedge with "may" — "may help support digestive health" is correct; "improves digestion" is not
- Do not say "boosts immunity" — this is not an approved health claim in Canada
- Do not say "live and active cultures" → use "active cultures" or name the specific strain (L. casei Danone)
- Do not say "live bacteria" — use "active cultures" or "active bacterial cultures"
- Do not position DanActive as a cure or treatment for any condition
- "High protein" — only if the product being referenced actually contains significant protein; do not assume

## Vocabulary — Never Use for DanActive

- live cultures (use "active cultures")
- boosts immunity
- probiotic supplement
- treats / cures (any medical framing)
- live bacteria
