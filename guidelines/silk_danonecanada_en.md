Language: Canadian English

---

## Brand Voice

- Do not disparage dairy or lactose-free products — stay neutral or favour the flexitarian stance
- Sustainability: be cautious — do not over-claim on the "green" side of plant-based beverages (PBB); a commissioned study shows PBB can be worse than Canadian milk for water consumption

## Product Naming

- Do not use "milk" or "cream" → prefer "almond base", "almond-based beverage", or "plant-based beverage"
  - Exception: "almond milk" is tolerated in meta-titles and metadata only
- "creamer" is acceptable for coffee whiteners
- Do not say "plant-based yogurt" as a product name → say "style yogurt"
- "Nextmilk" — do not capitalize "milk" in the brand name
- "Drink yogurt" → use "drinkable yogurt"
- "Additives" → use "preservatives"

## Nutrition Claims

- Do not use "healthy food" → only "healthy lifestyle" or "healthy way of life"
- "High protein" — never hyphenated
- Be careful with "rich" (in nutrients, calcium, etc.) → prefer "contains" or "source of…"
- Prefer "free of GMOs" or "GMO-free" over "Non-GMO Project verified"
- "Plant-based" cannot be used as a general descriptor

## Ingredients & Recipes

- Recipes using plant-based yogurt must always state "plant-based yogurt"
- Never use the word "treat"

## Vocabulary — Never Use for Silk

- milk (standalone product descriptor)
- cream
- healthy food
- sustainability (over-claiming)
