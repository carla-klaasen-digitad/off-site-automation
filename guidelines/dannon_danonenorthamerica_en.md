## **1. Brand Overview**

**Brand:** Dannon® (United States)
**Parent Company:** Danone North America, LLC (Certified B Corporation™)
**Industry Sector:** Dairy / Yogurt
**Market Area:** United States
**Founded:** 1942

**Brand Origin:**
Founded in 1942 by Isaac Carasso with a single guiding purpose: "bring health through food to as many people as possible." In 1947, Dannon introduced Fruit on the Bottom — the first fruit-flavored yogurt in the United States. The brand has maintained its foundational mission for over 80 years.

**Core Product Lines:**

* **Whole Milk Yogurt** — Plain (32 oz), Vanilla (32 oz)
* **Low Fat Yogurt** — Plain (5.3 oz, 32 oz), Vanilla (5.3 oz, 32 oz), Coffee (5.3 oz)
* **Nonfat Yogurt** — Plain (32 oz)
* **Fruit on the Bottom** — Blueberry, Strawberry, Peach (5 oz cups; heritage since 1947)
* **Creamy Classic** — Peach Mango, Strawberry, Strawberry Banana, Vanilla
* **Dannon Smoothie Yogurt Drink** — Mango, Strawberry, Strawberry Banana, Pina Colada

**Mission (Mandatory to Reflect in Content):**
Bringing health through food to as many people as possible.

## **3. Target Audiences**

### **Primary Target — Everyday Families**

* Age: 25–55, skews toward parents and household decision-makers
* Health-conscious but practical — not wellness extremists
* Value familiarity, trust, and heritage
* Looking for versatile, dependable yogurt for breakfast, snacks, and cooking
* WIC participants — Nonfat Plain, Whole Milk Plain, and Low Fat Plain are WIC-approved (state-dependent)

### **Secondary Target — Health-Aware Adults**

* Interested in gut health, probiotics, and digestion
* Managing weight or blood sugar (plain varieties appeal here)
* Appreciate the FDA-qualified health claim on plain low fat and nonfat varieties (type 2 diabetes risk reduction)
* Lactose-sensitive: yogurt contains less lactose than milk, making it easier to digest

### **Tertiary Target — Healthcare Professionals**

* Dedicated section on dannon.com
* Nutritional credibility and scientific grounding matter
* FDA health claims are especially relevant for this audience

## **7. Editorial Tone & Style**

### **Tone to Use**

* Warm
* Family-oriented
* Trustworthy and credible
* Mission-driven without being preachy
* Accessible and approachable
* Science-informed but written for everyday consumers
* Grounded in heritage and simplicity

### **Tone to Avoid**

* Overly clinical or nutritional-report style
* Exaggerated health claims or superlatives
* Fear-based messaging
* Moralizing about food choices
* Trendy wellness jargon that oversells

The brand leads with simplicity and trust — 80 years of doing one thing well. Do not overcomplicate.

## **8. Vocabulary Guidelines**

### **Preferred Terms**

* Live & active cultures
* Simple ingredients
* Grade A milk
* Cane sugar (when sweetener is mentioned)
* Good source of calcium / protein (when nutritionally accurate)
* Excellent source of calcium (plain low fat and nonfat — 20–25% DV)
* Good source of Vitamin D (flavored and fruit varieties)
* Non-GMO Project Verified
* Gluten-free certified
* Kosher
* WIC-approved (plain varieties, with state-dependent caveat)
* Less lactose than milk
* "Simply delicious since 1942" (approved tagline)
* B Corporation™ / Certified B Corp (parent company level only — Danone North America, not Dannon brand)

### **Words to Avoid or Exclude**

Never Use:
* Healthy (as a product claim)
* The best / #1
* Natural (use specific ingredient descriptions instead)
* Guilt-free
* Probiotic (Dannon does not market live cultures as "probiotics" — use "live & active cultures" instead)
* Sugar-free (no Dannon product makes this claim)
* Organic (Dannon is NOT organic — no USDA Organic certification)

⚠️ Important: Do NOT use the word "probiotic" for Dannon. The brand uses "live & active cultures." This is a key distinction from Activia, which does carry probiotic positioning.

Do not imply medical treatment or cures. The FDA health claim for plain varieties (type 2 diabetes risk reduction) has specific approved language — use it precisely or reference it generally without paraphrasing.

## **9. Compliance & Mandatory Inclusions**

All content must:

* Use "live & active cultures" — never "probiotics" for this brand
* Apply GFCO Gluten-Free claim accurately (brand-wide, certified)
* Apply Non-GMO Project Verified claim accurately (brand-wide)
* Apply FDA health claim language for type 2 diabetes ONLY to: Low Fat Plain and Nonfat Plain varieties — never to flavored products
* Note "Pina Colada Smoothie is naturally and artificially flavored" if that product is featured — it is the sole exception to Dannon's no-artificial-flavors stance
* Reference B Corp only at the parent company (Danone North America) level — Dannon itself is not separately B Corp certified
* WIC eligibility applies only to: Nonfat Plain, Whole Milk Plain, Low Fat Plain — state-dependent, include caveat
* Calcium claims by product type:
  * Plain Low Fat (32 oz): Excellent source — 20% DV
  * Plain Nonfat: Excellent source — 25% DV
  * Fruit on the Bottom: Good source — 15% DV
  * Other flavored varieties: Good source — 15% DV

**Standard FDA nutritional claim compliance applies throughout.**

## **11. Content Structure Requirements**

**Priority order for content production:**
1. Category pages (plain yogurt, low fat yogurt, whole milk yogurt, nonfat yogurt)
2. Product pages (one per SKU/format)
3. Other content (recipe, educational, FAQ)

### **Category Pages**

* SEO-optimized H1 targeting the category keyword (e.g., "Low Fat Yogurt")
* Education-first intro: what the category is, who it's for, how it fits daily life
* Highlight key certifications (Non-GMO, Gluten-Free, Kosher) and shared claims (live & active cultures, cane sugar only)
* Link to all product variants within the category
* Include recipe or use-case section (cooking, snacking, smoothies)
* FAQ section — 3 questions minimum

### **Product Pages**

* SEO-optimized H1 (product name + format, e.g., "Low Fat Plain Yogurt")
* Intro — 2–3 sentences; lead with simplicity and taste, then nutrition
* Feature bullets (3–5):
  * **Live & Active Cultures** — S. thermophilus and L. bulgaricus (add L. acidophilus for plain varieties)
  * **Non-GMO Project Verified**
  * **[Calcium claim]** — Excellent or Good source depending on variety
  * **No Artificial Colors or Flavors** (omit for Pina Colada Smoothie)
  * **Cane Sugar Only** (flavored varieties)
  * **WIC-Approved** (plain varieties only, with state-dependent caveat)
* FAQ section — exactly **3 questions** per product page
  * Cover: lactose content, cultures/probiotics distinction, use in cooking, certifications, sugar source
* FDA health claim copy (plain low fat and nonfat only): use precise approved language

### **Recipe & Blog Content**

* Lead with the dish or occasion, not the product
* Savory and sweet applications equally — reinforce "beyond the spoon" versatility
* Simple ingredient lists match the brand's core philosophy
* Categories: breakfast, snacks, smoothies, savory cooking (dips, marinades, sauces), baking
