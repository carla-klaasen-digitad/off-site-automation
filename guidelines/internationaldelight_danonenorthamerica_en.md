### FAQ Rules by Page Type
| Page type | Has FAQ? | Heading format |
|---|---|---|
| Individual product pages (flavor SKUs) | ✅ Yes | "Frequently Asked Questions About Our [Product]" or "[Flavor] Frequently Asked Questions" |
| Product-line / category pages (e.g., Iced Coffee) | ✅ Yes | Conversational: "All Your [Product] Questions Answered!" |
| Broad collection pages (e.g., top-level Coffee Creamers) | ❌ No | — |
| Recipe pages | ✅ Yes | "Questions Related to the [Recipe]" |

- **Always 3 questions** per FAQ section (unless the existing page already has more — maintain the existing count)
- Typical topics for product pages: taste profile, calorie count, sugar content, ingredients/allergens, use in baking/cooking
- Typical topics for recipe pages: variations, substitutions, storage, serving suggestions

### Product Page Content Structure (Individual SKUs)
1. **H1** — product name (e.g., "Zero Sugar French Vanilla Coffee Creamer")
2. **Intro paragraph** — 1–2 sentences; playful, warm; flavor-forward
3. **FAQ section** — 3 questions (see FAQ rules above)
4. **Nutritional Information** — serving size + full nutrition panel
5. **Ingredients list**

### Product-Line / Category Page Content Structure
1. **Punchy headline** — playful, fun, second-person questions or exclamations
2. **Short intro** — 2–3 sentences; inviting, warm, slightly whimsical; highlight flavor variety and formats
3. **Product range description** — mention flavors and formats; conversational
4. **Closing invitation** — direct CTA phrasing ("Pour, stir, and enjoy your cup your way")
5. **FAQ section** — 3 questions (some existing pages may have more)

### Recipe Page Content Structure
1. **Intro** — brief, flavor-forward; mentions the ID product used
2. **Tips section** — "Tips Before Making Your [Recipe]" with H3 subheadings
3. **Recipe block** — Prep time, total time, servings, Ingredients list, Directions (numbered steps)
4. **FAQ section** — "Questions Related to the [Recipe]" — 3 questions
   - Cover: variations, substitutions, storage, serving suggestions

- Avoid: clinical language, overly serious nutrition claims, children’s brand voice
