Language: Canadian English

---

## Brand Voice

- Evian is a premium natural spring water brand — tone should be calm and understated, never effusive
- Do not use "the healthiest water" or comparative superiority claims
- Sustainability is a real brand value but do not over-claim on environmental impact

## Product Naming

- "natural spring water" — the correct descriptor; never "purified water", "mineral water", or "filtered water"
- "glass bottled water" — acceptable for the glass range
- "still water" and "sparkling water" — acceptable product descriptors
- Do not say "alkaline water" — evian has a specific mineral composition but is not marketed as alkaline
- Do not say "detox water" — no detox properties should be attributed

## Claims

- "naturally occurring electrolytes" — acceptable (they occur naturally from the geological filtration process)
- Never claim evian "hydrates better than" other waters
- Do not say "pure" as an absolute claim ("natural" is the correct framing)
- Mineral composition may be referenced but don't turn it into a medical claim

## Vocabulary — Never Use for Evian

- purified
- alkaline (in a health claim context)
- detox / detoxifying
- the healthiest
- better hydration (comparative without evidence)
