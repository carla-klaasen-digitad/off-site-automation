### Product/Category Page Content Structure
1. **Intro paragraph** — 2–3 sentences; age/stage context, key certifications (USDA Organic, non-GMO, Clean Label Project)
2. **Certifications/feature list** — bullet or inline: Organic, non-GMO, no toxic persistent pesticides, no antibiotics, no artificial flavors
3. **Product range** — brief description of variants (Stage 1, Stage 2 for formula; flavor varieties for purees)
4. **FAQ section** — always present on category and product pages; see format below
5. **Learning Center CTA** — link to relevant expert articles

### FAQ Section Format — Required on Product/Category Pages
Use heading: **"Got More Questions about our [Product]?"** or **"Got Questions about Our [Product]? We've Got Answers!"**

- **3 questions** per page is standard; if the existing page already has more, maintain the existing count
- Standard topics: when/how to start (developmental milestone context), storage and safety (shelf life, refrigeration, spoon-feeding), ingredients, format/packaging specifics (jars vs. pouches, serving sizes)

> ⚠️ **Do NOT write allergy-friendly FAQ questions** (e.g., "Are Happy Baby snacks allergy-friendly?"). Happy Family has product lines specifically designed to *introduce* allergens (e.g., Nutty Blends™), so blanket allergy-free claims are inaccurate.

**Storage FAQ language:**
- For storage questions, use: *"Please see the instructions on our product packaging to learn about proper storage."*
- If elaborating on refrigeration, use: *"Most of our baby food jars and pouches can be kept in the refrigerator for up to 2–3 days. Please check the product label to see how long it can keep in the refrigerator."*
- Do **not** write "refrigerator for up to 3 days" without the 2–3 day range and product label caveat.

> ✅ **FAQs are required on Happy Family product and category pages** — this distinguishes Happy Family from Oikos (no FAQs on product pages).
