## **1. Brand Overview**

**Brand:** Danone Away From Home (United States)
**Parent Company:** Danone North America (Certified B Corporation™)
**Industry Sector:** B2B Foodservice / Operator Sales
**Market Area:** United States
**Audience Type:** Operators and institutional buyers — NOT consumers

**Description:**
Danone Away From Home is Danone North America's B2B foodservice division, connecting operators with the full portfolio of Danone brands. It supports foodservice directors, procurement teams, and nutrition managers across key institutional channels with branded products, operational tools, and business development resources.

**Core Portfolio (Operator-Facing):**

* **Yogurt & Dairy:** Oikos®, Dannon®, Activia®, Light+Fit®, Danimals®, YoCrunch®, Two Good®
* **Plant-Based:** Silk®, So Delicious® Dairy Free
* **Creamers & Coffee:** International Delight®, Land O'Lakes®, SToK®
* **Equipment Programs:** Soft-serve and frozen dessert dispenser equipment for eligible operators

**Operator Channels:**
1. Healthcare (hospitals, long-term care, senior living)
2. Education (K-12, colleges and universities)
3. Business Dining (corporate cafeterias, workplace food programs)
4. Hotels & Lodging
5. Recreation (stadiums, arenas, theme parks)
6. Corrections
7. Military

**Mission (Mandatory to Reflect in Content):**
"Bring health through food to as many people as possible." — channeled through institutional and foodservice partnerships that extend Danone's reach beyond retail.

## **3. Target Audiences**

### **Primary Target — Foodservice Operators & Procurement Teams**

* Foodservice directors, nutrition managers, executive chefs in institutional settings
* Procurement and category managers at healthcare systems, school districts, universities, hotel chains

* Registered dietitians in healthcare and education foodservice

* Activia, Dannon, Silk, and So Delicious all have nutritional positioning relevant here

## **7. Editorial Tone & Style**

### **Tone to Use**

* Professionally warm
* Credibility-led — lead with institutional trust signals (B Corp, portfolio breadth, proven brands)
* Operator-focused — speak to business outcomes, not consumer taste experience
* Mission-driven without being preachy
* Solutions-oriented — this brand solves operational problems, not just flavor preferences
* Specific and factual — operators respond to data, certifications, and tangible program details

### **Tone to Avoid**

* Consumer-facing language (flavor excitement or sensory descriptions as the lead)
* Exaggerated superlatives
* Vague sustainability claims — cite B Corp, specific brand certifications, concrete commitments
* Clinical or academic writing — accessible professional writing, not scientific reports
* Promotional hype — operators want credibility, not marketing enthusiasm

---

## **8. Vocabulary Guidelines**

### **Preferred Terms**

* Operator / operators (not "customers" or "clients")
* Foodservice / foodservice solution
* Portfolio (full Danone portfolio)
* Channel (use channel-specific language: healthcare channel, education channel)
* B Corp certified / Certified B Corporation™ (Danone North America)
* Sustainable sourcing
* Plant-based (for Silk, So Delicious positioning)
* Dairy-free (for So Delicious specifically)
* On-trend / menu trends (connect Danone brands to operator menu strategy)

### **Words to Avoid or Exclude**

Never Use:
* Healthy (as a product claim)
* The best / #1
* Natural (use specific sourcing descriptions)
* Guilt-free
* Probiotic (use "live & active cultures" when referencing yogurt brands)
* Consumer-facing superlatives or emotional triggers

⚠️ This is a B2B brand. Avoid consumer-facing copy patterns entirely. Speak to operators, not shoppers.

## **9. Compliance & Mandatory Inclusions**

All content must:

* Apply B Corp claim only at the Danone North America level — not individual brand level
* Reference individual brand certifications accurately: each product line carries its own certifications (see individual brand guidelines for Oikos, Dannon, Silk, So Delicious, etc.)
* Not make nutritional health claims about products unless referencing the specific product's approved claim
* Use ™ or ® for brand names on first use: Oikos®, Dannon®, Silk®, So Delicious®, International Delight®, SToK®, YoCrunch®

**Standard FDA nutritional claim compliance applies throughout.**

## **11. Content Structure Requirements**

**Priority order for content production:**
1. Channel pages (one per segment: Healthcare, Education, Business Dining, Hotels & Lodging, Recreation, Corrections, Military)
2. Portfolio/brand pages (Danone brands framed for operator use cases)
3. Resource pages (nutrition specs, equipment program info, operator tools)
4. Educational/blog content (B Corp procurement, plant-based menu trends, category insights)

### **Channel Pages**

* SEO-optimized H1 targeting the operator segment + foodservice category
* Lead with the operational challenge or need for that channel (e.g., "Meeting diverse dietary needs in healthcare foodservice")
* Highlight relevant Danone brands and products for that channel
* Include B Corp and sustainability messaging where relevant to procurement in that sector
* CTA: Contact a rep / Request samples / Explore portfolio

### **Portfolio/Brand Pages**

* Frame each Danone brand through an operator lens — menu applications, customer recognition, format availability
* Note certifications and dietary credentials relevant to operator specifications
* Link to individual product specs where available

### **Resource/Tools Pages**

* Nutrition data, allergen charts, and product spec sheets — clean, accessible format for dietitians and procurement
* Equipment program details (dispenser programs) — eligibility, process, supported products

### **Educational Content**

* Lead with operator insight or industry trend
* Connect to Danone portfolio as a solution
* Cite real credentials (B Corp, Rainforest Alliance, specific certifications) — no vague claims
