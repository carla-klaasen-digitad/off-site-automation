## Brand Overview

Danone Yogurt Canada is the Canadian yogurt brand under Danone, best known for Danino — a probiotic drinkable yogurt marketed to children. Website: danoneyogurt.ca

## Product Naming

- Brand name: "Danone Yogurt" or "Danino" (for the kids' drinkable yogurt line)
- "drinkable yogurt" — the correct product descriptor
- "kids' yogurt" or "yogurt for children" — acceptable
- Do not say "yogurt drink" — use "drinkable yogurt"
- Do not say "probiotic supplement" — Danino is a food, not a supplement
- Do not say "Dannon" — that is the US brand; the Canadian brand is "Danone Yogurt"

## Claims

- Probiotic claim rule: always hedge with "may" — "may help support digestive health" is correct; "improves digestion" is not
- Do not say "boosts immunity" — not an approved health claim in Canada
- Do not say "live cultures" → use "active cultures"
- Do not position the product as a cure or treatment for any condition
- Do not make claims about the product supporting children's development or growth without verified data
- Calcium and vitamin D may be mentioned as nutrients present in the product, without making disease claims

## Audience

- Primary audience: parents shopping for children's snacks and lunches
- Tone when referencing the audience: practical, reassuring — not alarmist or pushy
- Do not say children "need" the product — frame as a convenient, simple option

## Vocabulary — Never Use for Danone Yogurt

- live cultures (use "active cultures")
- boosts immunity
- probiotic supplement
- builds strong bones (implied disease claim)
- Dannon (US brand name)
