## **1. Brand Overview**

**Brand:** Follow Your Heart® (United States)
**Parent Company:** Earth Island
**Industry Sector:** Plant-Based / Dairy-Free Foods
**Market Area:** United States
**Founded:** 1970, Canoga Park, Southern California

**Brand Origin:**
Started as a 7-seat soup, salad, and sandwich counter in the back of a natural food store. Born from a need for egg-free mayo, co-founder Bob developed Vegenaise — their signature product and the foundation of the Earth Island manufacturing entity created to scale it.

**Core Product Lines:**

* **Vegenaise®** — Dairy-free, egg-free mayo (Original, Avocado Oil, Chipotle, Organic, Grapeseed Oil, Soy-Free)
* **Dairy-Free Cheese Slices** — Mozzarella, Medium Cheddar, Pepper Jack, Provolone, American, Smoked Gouda
* **Dairy-Free Cheese Shreds** — Mozzarella Style, Cheddar Style
* **Dairy-Free Cheese Crumbles & Specialty** — Feta, Bleu Cheese, Parmesan Shredded, Parmesan Grated
* **Dairy-Free Salad Dressings** — Ranch, Bleu Cheese, Honey Mustard Style, Organic Caesar (sub-brand: Live Healthy®)
* **Dairy-Free Sour Cream**
* **Dairy-Free Yogurt** — Rich & creamy with active probiotic cultures; 10 flavors

**Mission (Mandatory to Reflect in Content):**
"To produce high-quality plant-based foods to enhance the lives of consumers and contribute to the betterment of the Earth and all its inhabitants."

**Core Brand Philosophy:** Food should taste good and do good.

## **3. Target Audiences**

### **Primary Target — Committed Plant-Based Consumers**

* Vegans and long-term plant-based eaters
* Value-driven: ethics, sustainability, and animal welfare motivate purchasing
* Functionally demanding: expect plant-based products to perform like their dairy/egg counterparts
* Brand-loyal once trust is established

### **Secondary Target — Flexitarians & Allergen-Avoiders**

* Reducing dairy or eggs for health, environmental, or ethical reasons
* Dairy-free (lactose intolerance, dairy allergy) and egg-free consumers
* Soy-free consumers (addressed by Soy-Free Vegenaise, Avocado Oil Vegenaise, Chipotle Vegenaise, all cheese products)
* Kosher consumers (all products KOF-K Kosher Parve)

### **Tertiary Target — Family Shoppers**

* Parents making plant-based transitions for the household
* Kids appeal: American-style slices specifically positioned as mild and kid-friendly
* Inclusive messaging: "even your non-vegan friends will love it"

## **7. Editorial Tone & Style**

### **Tone to Use**

* Warm
* Mission-driven
* Quietly passionate
* Purposeful
* Approachable and inclusive
* Confident without being preachy
* Real and grounded — rooted in a genuine 50-year story

### **Tone to Avoid**

* Preachy or guilt-tripping (about meat/dairy consumption)
* Overly clinical or nutritional-report style
* Exaggerated health claims or superlatives
* Fear-based messaging
* Exclusionary language (content should welcome flexitarians and curious consumers, not just committed vegans)

Do not lecture readers about their food choices. The brand leads by example and by flavor.

---

## **8. Vocabulary Guidelines**

### **Preferred Terms**

* Plant-based
* Dairy-free
* Egg-free
* Vegan
* Soy-free (when product qualifies)
* Live & active cultures (yogurt)
* Expeller-pressed (oils)
* Made with [specific oil/ingredient]
* Non-GMO Project Verified
* Good source of calcium (cheese products — 10% DV per serving; Parmesan 20% DV)
* 100% renewable electricity / powered by renewable energy
* Better Than Mayo® (Vegenaise — registered tagline)

### **Words to Avoid or Exclude**

Never Use:
* Healthy (as a product claim)
* The best (superlative)
* Natural (use "naturally sourced" or specific ingredient descriptions)
* Guilt-free
* Clean (as in "clean eating" — too vague)
* Dairy alternative (use "dairy-free")

Do not make disease prevention or treatment claims. Calcium and Vitamin D claims are acceptable when nutritionally accurate per serving.

## **9. Compliance & Mandatory Inclusions**

All content must:

* Use registered trademark symbol: Vegenaise® on first use per piece
* Reference Non-GMO Project Verified status accurately — apply only when confirmed for that product
* Not claim any product is nut-free — coconut is present in Sour Cream and some cheese products; cross-contamination risk exists
* Apply USDA Organic claim only to: Organic Vegenaise and Organic Caesar Dressing
* Apply Rainforest Alliance claim only to: Parmesan products
* Reference "100% renewable electricity" for manufacturing claims only — not a product-level claim
* Use "vegan honey alternative" or "no honey" for Honey Mustard dressing — it contains no animal honey
* Respect soy-free distinction: only Avocado Oil, Chipotle, and Soy-Free Vegenaise varieties are soy-free; all cheese products are soy-free; Ranch, Bleu Cheese, Honey Mustard, Caesar dressings contain soy

**Standard FDA nutritional claim compliance applies throughout.**

## **11. Content Structure Requirements**

### **Product Pages**

* SEO-optimized H1 (include product name + dairy-free/vegan descriptor)
* Intro paragraph — 2–3 sentences; lead with taste and performance, then call out key certification (vegan, gluten-free, Non-GMO)
* Feature bullets (3–5):
  * **[Key attribute]** — e.g., "Soy-Free Formula" / "USDA Organic" / "Expeller-Pressed Avocado Oil"
  * **Non-GMO Project Verified** — call out on all products
  * **Dairy-Free & Vegan** — consistent across all
  * **Gluten-Free** — consistent across all
  * **Good Source of Calcium** — for cheese products (10% DV); Parmesan at 20% DV
* FAQ section — exactly **3 questions** per product page
  * Cover: allergens (soy-free? nut-free?), certifications, use-case/cooking functionality
* Trademark disclosure: Vegenaise® on first use

### **Recipe Pages**

* Lead with the dish, not the product
* Include "Client Information" block for social captions if requested
* Recipe hub categories: Breakfast, Main Dishes, Appetizers, Desserts, Sauces, Vegenaise-specific
* Occasion and lifestyle tags: Vegan, Gluten-Free, Soy-Free where applicable

### **Blog / Educational Articles**

* Lead with the consumer question or pain point
* Science and ingredient explanations should be accessible — not clinical
* Sustainability content should feel earned and factual (cite solar output, Rainforest Alliance, renewable energy)
* Recipe integration encouraged in educational pieces

### **Category / Landing Pages**

* Lead with the product family's differentiator (Vegenaise → "the original better mayo"; Cheese → taste + functionality)
* List all active SKUs with clear soy-free/organic callouts
* Include store-locator CTA

## **12. Emotional & Brand Perception Goals**

The reader should feel:

* That better-tasting plant-based food is genuinely possible
* Welcomed — whether fully vegan or just curious
* Informed and confident about ingredients and certifications
* Connected to a brand that has been doing this for 50+ years

The brand should be perceived as:

* A pioneer — not a trend follower
* Honest and transparent
* Mission-driven without being preachy
* Trustworthy through specificity (cite real certifications, real sustainability numbers)

## **13. Level of Detail**

Medium.

* Provide enough specificity to build trust (ingredient sourcing, certification names, nutritional facts).
* Avoid academic or overly technical writing.
* Keep recipe and lifestyle content light and accessible.
* Sustainability content should include real numbers (2,000 solar panels, 300,000+ kWh/year, 100% renewable electricity since 2024) — not vague claims.
