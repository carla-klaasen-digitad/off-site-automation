## **7. Editorial Tone & Style**

### **Tone to Use**

* Playful and spirited
* Soul-forward (brand uses "soulful" and "feed your soul" language)
* Mission-driven without lecturing
* Inclusive and welcoming
* Grounded in genuine sustainability commitments
* Confident — the brand has been doing this for 30+ years

### **Tone to Avoid**

* Preachy or guilt-tripping (about dairy consumption)
* Health-claim heavy — this brand leads with taste and values, not nutrition
* Vague sustainability language — cite real commitments (B Corp, Rainforest Alliance, living wage)
* Clinical or ingredient-list-heavy intros
* Exaggerated superlatives

Do not imply So Delicious products are nutritionally equivalent to dairy. Lead with taste, texture, and mission.

## **8. Vocabulary Guidelines**

### **Preferred Terms**

* Dairy-free your mind (tagline — use sparingly, not in every piece)
* Feed your soul (frozen desserts sub-tagline)
* More Than The Minimum (mission language)
* Certified Vegan
* Non-GMO Project Verified
* Gluten-free certified
* Made with organic coconut (coconutmilk products)
* Rainforest Alliance certified cocoa (chocolate products)
* B Corp certified / B Corporation
* Live and active cultures (yogurt alternatives)
* No added sugar (for NSA products — use exact phrasing)
* CocoWhip™ (registered product name — use ™)
* Dairy free (two words, no hyphen in brand name; hyphenated in category terms like "dairy-free yogurt")

### **Words to Avoid or Exclude**

Never Use:
* Healthy (as a product claim)
* The best / #1
* Natural (use specific ingredient or sourcing descriptions)
* Guilt-free
* Probiotic (use "live and active cultures" for yogurt)
* Sugar-free (use "no added sugar" or "0g added sugar")
* Dairy alternative (use "dairy-free")
* Milk alternative (use the specific base: "coconutmilk," "cashewmilk," etc.)

⚠️ Coconutmilk, cashewmilk, almondmilk are written as **one word** — this is So Delicious brand style (matches website usage).

## **11. Content Structure Requirements**

**Priority order for content production:**
1. Frozen dessert category/product pages
2. Yogurt alternative pages
3. Beverage pages
4. Creamer pages
5. Recipe and blog content

### **Product Pages**

* SEO-optimized H1 (product name + base type, e.g., "Cashewmilk Frozen Dessert — Salted Caramel Cluster")
* Intro — 2–3 sentences; lead with texture and flavor experience, then certifications
* Feature bullets (3–5):
  * **Certified Vegan** — consistent across all
  * **Non-GMO Project Verified** — consistent across all
  * **Gluten-Free Certified** — consistent across all
  * **[Base type]** — e.g., "Made with cashewmilk" / "Made with organic coconutmilk"
  * **Rainforest Alliance Certified Cocoa** (chocolate products only)
  * **No Added Sugar** (NSA products only)
* FAQ section — exactly **3 questions** per product page
  * Cover: allergens (soy? nut? coconut?), certifications, No Sugar Added sweetener questions, storage

### **Recipe Pages**

* Lead with the dish or occasion
* Highlight dairy-free substitution angle — So Delicious as a 1:1 swap
* Tag by lifestyle: Vegan, Gluten-Free, No Sugar Added where applicable
* CocoWhip recipes: dessert toppings, holiday entertaining, smoothie bowls

### **Blog / Educational Content**

* Lead with consumer question (e.g., "What is coconutmilk ice cream?")
* Sustainability content: cite real data — B Corp, Rainforest Alliance, living wage, renewable electricity
* Dairy-free substitutions guide — position products as seamless everyday swaps
* Do not oversell nutrition — this brand's edge is values + taste, not health claims

### **Category / Landing Pages**

* Lead with the widest variety claim — most bases, most flavors
* Organize by base type for frozen desserts (cashewmilk, coconutmilk, oatmilk, almondmilk)
* Include "Where to Buy" and Flavor Quiz CTAs
